Srijita Bandopadhyay

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Computational Mathematics, Science, and Engineering at Michigan State University. I am a Graduate Research Assistant in the SLIM Group, advised by Saiprasad Ravishankar.

My research sits at the intersection of machine learning, optimization, 3D reconstruction, inverse problems, and computational imaging. I develop data-efficient methods for image restoration and reconstruction from sparse, noisy, or low-dose measurements.

Before joining MSU, I earned my Bachelor's degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering with a minor in Computer Science from the University of Engineering and Management, Kolkata. During my undergraduate studies, I was supervised by Prof. Soumen Banerjee. I worked on deep learning and computer vision applications.

Research

I work on optimization-inspired reconstruction models that combine unrolled algorithms, learned priors, sparsity regularization, and deep image priors. I am especially interested in settings where clean targets, dense measurements, or paired training data are difficult to obtain.
